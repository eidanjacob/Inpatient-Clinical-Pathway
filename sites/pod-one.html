Post-Operative Day 1

Pain Management
Managed by the Acute Pain Management Service
PCA Diazepam Acetaminophen Gabapentin Oxycodone NSAIDs
POD 0 Demand Only PO/IV q8h IV q6h ATC x 24h Alternate with Acetaminophen PO TID ATC
POD 1 Demand Only PO q8h PRN PO/IV PRN Ibuprofen q6h ATC PO TID ATC q6h ATC
POD 2 D/C consider PRN opiods PO q8h PRN PO PRN Ibuprofen q6h ATC PO TID ATC ATC
POD 3 PRN opiods PO q8h PRN PO PRN Ibuprofen q6h ATC PO TID ATC ATC
  • Diazepam 0.05-0.1mg/kg/dose PO/IV (PRN may be switched to ATC postop)
    Alternative to diazepam - Methocarbamol: 15mg/kg IV PRN x1 (PACU only), 10mg/kg PO q6h PRN
  • Acetaminophen 10-15mg/kg/dose PO/IV (i.e. MN-6AM-12PM-6PM)
  • Ketorolac 0.5mg/kg/dose (i.e. 3PM-9PM-3AM)
  • Ibuprofen 5-10mg/kg/dose (i.e. 9A-3P-9P-etc)
  • Oxycodone 0.05-0.15mg/kg dose (Tramadol 1-1.5mg/kg/dose as alternative)
  • Nalbuphine 0.05-0.1mg/kg/dose IV q3h prn for pruritus (central opioid)
  • Gabapentin dosing: >50 kg 200mg TID, < 50 kg5mg /kg TID. Stop at discharge. No home prescription or wean necessary unless rare exception
Activity/PT
  • Log roll q2 hours and PRN until patient can roll independently
  • Start PT and OT daily
  • Start ambulation TID
  • OOB to chair TID
  • Use brace as needed, if ordered and available
  • SCDs while in bed and asleep
  • Out of bed to chair
Nutrition
  • Advance diet as tolerated
  • Encourage sugar-free gum chewing
  • Discontinue IVF once patient tolerating clears — saline lock PIV
Tubes/Drains
  • Maintain foley catheter and record urine output Q8 hours until foley removal (remove at noon if clinically appropriate)
  • Record surgical drain output q8 hours
  • Reinforce surgical incision site/surgical drain dressings as needed
  • If surgical incision site dressing becomes saturated, notify MD/NP
  • DC Foley as clinically appropriate
Respiratory
  • Incentive spirometry q2 while awake
Medication Management
  • Complete 3 doses post-operative antibiotic prophylaxis
  • Continue bowel regimen as ordered
Laboratory
  • CBC, Chem
  • Scoliosis X-rays — if ordered, complete once patient is able to tolerate prior to discharge
Discharge Planning
  • Assess home health & transportation needs
  • Provide family with written needs
